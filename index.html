Sweet pitayas have a creamy pulp and a delicate aroma. The fruit's texture is sometimes likened to that of the kiwifruit because of its black, crunchy seeds. These fruits are commonly known in English as "dragon fruit", reflecting its vernacular Asian names. Pitahaya-producing cacti of the genus Hylocereus are originally native to Mexico. They were transplanted to Central America and to other parts of the world.

Preparation:

To prepare a pitaya for consumption, the fruit is cut open to expose the flesh. The flesh, which is eaten raw, is mildly sweet and low in calories. The seeds are eaten together with the flesh, have a nutty taste and are rich in lipids, but they are indigestible unless chewed.

Ingredients: pitaya fruit (hylocereus undatus)

The prickly pear plant has three different edible sections: the pad of the cactus (nopal), which can be treated like a vegetable, the petals of the flowers, which can be added to salads, and the pear (tuna), which can be treated like a fruit. They grow wild throughout the American southwest, down to South America and up to Canada. The ones you may find at a local store or farmers market will surely originate from a commercial nopal farm.

Preparation:

cut off both ends of the fruit, carve away the thorny skin, remove the seeds, and enjoy!

Ingredients: prickly pear fruit
